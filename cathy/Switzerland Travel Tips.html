Welcome to Switzerland Travel Tips

When to Go

There is no bad time to visit Switzerland! It just depends on what you are looking for during your trip. If you're looking for a holiday full of winter sports, then winter is the best time to go. Between November and February, the snow season is at its peak. Skiing and snowboarding are incredible, and all of Switzerland looks like a winter wonderland.

Getting Around

Public transport is very well-maintained and easy to follow, and many locals speak English as well. So if you ever get lost or have a hard time finding a place you want to go, it's easy to get back on track.

  • Trains are the fastest and most efficient way to travel between cities. Buses are a bit slower and cheaper.
  • Renting a car is another option. The roads are easy to drive and well-maintained, though renting a car is still more expensive than public transport.
  • Bicycles can be taken on most SBB trains, private railways, and PostBuses as long as you buy a separate bike ticket or Day Bike Pass.

Accommodations

  • Book Early:

    -Especially in peak seasons, as popular spots can fill up quickly.

  • Consider Guesthouses:

    -They often offer a more authentic Swiss experience compared to hotels.

  • Campsites:

    -Switzerland has many beautiful campsites for a unique outdoor experience.

Safety and Health

  • Emergency Numbers:

    -Familiarize yourself with emergency services: 112 for general emergencies and 144 for medical emergencies.

  • Travel Insurance:

    -Always advisable for peace of mind, covering health and trip cancellations.
